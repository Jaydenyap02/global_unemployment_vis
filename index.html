Global Unemployment Rate

What is Unemployment Rate

unemployment rate is a key indicator of economic health. It represents the percentage of the labor force that is unemployed and actively seeking work. It provides insight into the economic challenges faced by a country and the ability of its labor market to absorb new and existing workers.

The goal of this visualization is to explore how unemployment rates have evolved globally between 1991 and 2021, highlighting countries with significant changes in unemployment, the average unemployment rates across different regions, and the relationship between human development and unemployment rates

How Unemployment Rate changing globally?

Over time, unemployment rates have fluctuated across the globe due to various factors. Most Asian nations, unemployment rates remained relatively low and stable throughout the 1990s and early 2000s.

However, events such as the 2008 global financial crisisled to a sharp rise in unemployment worldwide, with a slow recovery in many regions

COVID-19 pandamic in year 2020 also triggered another significant spike in unemployment rates.

Australia's Outstanding Performance

The unemployment rate in Australia has shown a fluctuating trend over the past few decades. From a high rate in the early 1990s, the unemployment rate gradually decreased, reflecting a strengthening economy. Despite the impact of global economic events, including the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, Australia's unemployment rate remained relatively stable, with a slight uptick during the peak of the crisis.
